● Disciplinary Procedure

The policy has been established so that the employee understands what course of action may be taken to assist the employee if the employee job related performance/ behavior falls below Management expectations.

The following is intended to be a guideline and is not intended to be all-inclusive, as

circumstances and incidents may vary. This guideline is not intended to limit the right of the company to initiate disciplinary action and or terminate employees at any time, at its sole discretion. Company reserves the right to change or modify the terms set forth at its discretion.

● Disciplinary Situations

Certain examples for initiating disciplinary action are:

1. Job performance far below required standards as outlined in the Key Result

Areas (KRA)

2. Attendance Punctuality or Working Hours

3. Violation of a company policy or Code of Conduct

When a disciplinary situation exists, Managers must take prompt action that is fair and consistent and takes into account the performance history of the employee without any prejudice. Managers must give the Employee to explain his/ her stand on the issue held accountable for.

● Corrective action

The company holds each of its employees to certain work rules and standards of conduct. When an employee deviates from these rules and standards, the company expects the employee’s reporting manager to take corrective action. Corrective action at the company is progressive. That is, the action taken in response to a rule infraction or violation of standards typically follows a pattern increasing in seriousness until the infraction or violation is corrected.The usual sequence of domestic enquiry includes an oral warning, a written warning, probation, and finally termination of employment. In deciding which initial corrective action would be appropriate, a supervisor will consider the seriousness of the infraction, the circumstances surrounding the matter, and the employee’s previous record. Though committed to a progressive approach to corrective action, IOT Monks Private Limited considers certain rule infractions and violations of standards as grounds for immediate termination of employment.

Any clarification on the seriousness or impact of the infraction or violation can be clarified by the Manager with the COC Committee.

● Protection against retaliation It is a violation of the policy to engage in retaliatory acts against any employee who reports any code of conduct violation or any employee who testifies, assists or participates in a proceeding, investigation or hearing relating to such allegation of harassmentEmployees who believe they have been retaliated against because of testifying, assisting or participating in a proceeding, investigation, or hearing relating to an allegation of Code of conduct violation, should meet with and seek the advice of the HR department, whose responsibilities include handling retaliation. If an employee believes that he or she has been retaliated against in the form of an adverse personnel action for disclosing Concern under the policy he/she may file a written Concern to the MD requesting an appropriate remedy.
